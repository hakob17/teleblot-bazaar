Rules
Bazaar blot is one of the varieties of blot, the main difference is the presence of trade before the draw. The game uses a deck of 32 cards (T-7). The goal of the game is to score 101, 201 or 301 points earlier than a couple of opponents will do.

In each hand, players receive 8 cards. Then an auction is held. The pair that makes the highest bet wins. Then there is a rally and scoring.

The right of the first word in the trade is the player to the left of the dealer. There are several trade features that need to be remembered.

The player who said "pass" may participate in the next round of betting.

The "Contra" application is made out of turn and means the end of the trade. After the application "Contra" the opponent can only answer "Rekurenti", and the bet can not change. The application "Contra" is made by one of the players at the opponents' bet and until the moment when the partner of the competitor makes any request. "Counter" multiplies the bet by 2.

The application of "Recontra" is made by one of the players of the pair, whose bet was terminated.

The application "Recontra" multiplies the bet by 4.

If at the beginning of the auction the application "pass" sounded 4 times, then the cards will be retaken

Trade opens with eight or more. The next application must be "previous + minimum 1". The colors in the blot are equal, the bets differ only in numbers.

If your partner made a bet, then you can raise it by stating the number and the same suit. "(South -" 8 peak ", West -" pass ", you -" 10 peak ")

In the Bazaar blot all games are the same number of points: 162 large or 16 small.

Kaput - a game in which the couple announcing it undertakes to take all bribes (8 pieces) with the appropriate trump. The cost of the game "Caput" is more than any other simple game: 25 points instead of 16.

After the application of Kaput, further trade is possible only with the announcement of Kaput.

After the end of the auction the right of the first move belongs to the player to the left of the dealer. Before his first move (before the card is placed on the table), each player declares his senior combination.

If both pairs have combinations, it turns out whose combination is older. The first player makes the first move, then all the other players make a move in turn. The bribe is taken by the player who placed the highest card in the suit stroke or the senior trump card. Taking the bribe The right of the next move belongs to the player who took the bribe.

Only before putting his card in the second trick the player must show his combination, if it is single or senior. If the combination has not been shown or shown at the wrong time, then it is considered void and the opponent can show his combination.

Rules of drawing:

If there are cards in the suit stroke - you need to play them. If there are no cards in the suit stroke, then you need to beat a trump if the current bribe already / yet belongs to the opponent, or you can fold any card if the current bribe already / still belongs to your pair. You can take a trump card, based on some of your considerations.

When playing trump suit, it is necessary to interrupt the card, if there is such a possibility, and it is necessary to interrupt the card of the partner.

In the event that you do not have cards in the suit stroke, and the opponent has beaten a bribe with a trump card, which you can not kill, then in this case you do not have to put a trump card, you can throw off any card.

The seniority of cards in a trump suit is 9 T 10 K D 8 7,

The seniority of cards in a noncross suit - T 10 K D B 9 8 7.
